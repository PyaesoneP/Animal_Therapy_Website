Resources and Support for Body Dysmorphia

If you are struggling with body dysmorphia, remember you are not alone. Here are some valuable resources and organizations that can provide support and information:

Organizations and Websites

  • The Body Dysmorphic Disorder Foundation (BDDF) - Provides information, support, and resources for individuals with BDD and their families.
  • International OCD Foundation (IOCDF) - BDD is related to OCD, and the IOCDF offers extensive information and resources on BDD.
  • Anxiety & Depression Association of America (ADAA) - Offers information on anxiety disorders, including BDD, and helps find treatment.

Finding Professional Help

It is crucial to seek professional help for body dysmorphia. Talk to your doctor or a mental health professional to get a proper diagnosis and discuss treatment options. You can also search online directories of therapists and mental health providers in your area.

  • Psychology Today Therapist Finder - A directory to search for therapists by location and specialization.

Support Groups and Communities

Connecting with others who understand BDD can be incredibly helpful. Look for online or in-person support groups and communities.

**Disclaimer:** This website provides information and simulated support, but it is not a substitute for professional medical or mental health treatment. If you are struggling, please reach out to a qualified professional.

About Us

Welcome to Pawsitive Pathways! We believe in the incredible power of animal-assisted therapy, especially the unconditional love and joy that dogs bring. Our team of trained therapists and adorable therapy dogs are dedicated to supporting individuals struggling with body dysmorphia.

Why Dog Therapy for Body Dysmorphia?

Dog therapy offers unique benefits for individuals dealing with body dysmorphia:

  • Unconditional Positive Regard: Dogs offer non-judgmental acceptance...
  • Reduced Self-Consciousness: Interacting with dogs shifts focus...
  • Sensory Comfort: Petting and cuddling dogs releases endorphins...
  • Emotional Regulation: Dogs can intuitively sense emotions...

We provide a safe, supportive, and uplifting environment where you can connect with our gentle dogs, explore your feelings, and build a healthier relationship with your body image. Our approach is compassionate, evidence-based, and always puppy-powered!

Our Therapy Sessions

Our therapy sessions are designed to be a warm and comforting experience. You'll spend time interacting with our specially trained therapy dogs in a calming and private setting. Studies show that interacting with animals can reduce stress, release endorphins, and improve mood. Our sessions incorporate these benefits to help you:

  • Reduce anxiety and stress related to body image.
  • Improve self-esteem and body confidence.
  • Develop healthier coping mechanisms.
  • Experience unconditional positive regard from our furry therapists.

Each session is tailored to your individual needs and goals. We combine talk therapy techniques with dog interaction to create a holistic and effective approach.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Body Dysmorphia?

Body dysmorphia is a mental health condition where a person spends a lot of time worrying about flaws in their appearance. These flaws are often unnoticeable to others.

How Does Dog Therapy Work?

Dog therapy, or animal-assisted therapy, uses the human-animal bond to provide comfort, reduce anxiety, and promote emotional well-being. Interacting with dogs can release endorphins and create a calming therapeutic environment.

What Happens in a Therapy Session?

Sessions typically involve spending time interacting with our trained therapy dogs in a comfortable setting. Therapists guide sessions to help you explore your feelings and develop coping strategies, alongside the therapeutic presence of the dogs.

Are Your Therapists Qualified?

Yes, all our therapists are licensed and experienced mental health professionals with specialized training in animal-assisted therapy. Meet our team here.

What are Your Fees?

Please contact us to inquire about our session fees and any available payment options. We are committed to making therapy accessible.

Is This Covered by Insurance?

Insurance coverage for animal-assisted therapy varies. We recommend checking with your insurance provider to understand your coverage options.

Understanding Body Dysmorphia

Body dysmorphic disorder (BDD) is a mental health condition where you can't stop thinking about one or more perceived defects or flaws in your appearance — a flaw that appears minor or can't be seen by others. But to you, these flaws are significant and cause you distress and impact your daily life.

Symptoms of BDD

  • Being extremely preoccupied with a perceived flaw in appearance that to others can't be seen or appears minor
  • Strongly believing that you have a defect in your appearance that makes you ugly or deformed
  • Engaging in behaviors aimed at fixing or hiding the perceived flaw that are hard to resist and difficult to control, such as frequently checking the mirror, grooming, or skin picking
  • Trying to hide perceived flaws with styling, makeup or clothes
  • Constantly comparing your appearance with others
  • Frequently seeking reassurance about your appearance from others

It's More Than Just Vanity

It's important to understand that BDD is not simply about being vain or overly concerned with appearance. It's a serious condition that can significantly impact mental well-being and quality of life.

Seeking Professional Help

If you believe you may be experiencing symptoms of body dysmorphia, it's important to seek professional help from a mental health professional. This website is for informational and supportive purposes and does not replace professional diagnosis and treatment.
